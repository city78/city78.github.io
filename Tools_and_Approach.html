About City78

We bring together data-driven community perception analysis with a cutting-edge urban planning approach to develop deep, nuanced understandings of neighborhoods and cities and to create actionable solutions that improve the quality of urban life for all.

Our Story

Answering tough questions to build resilient, community-driven cities

Private sector

City78 solves narrative and planning problems that face companies who are seeking guidance on situating their proespective development, monitoring public response to new projects, or understand key narratives or GIS patterns across the urban space.

Government & nonprofit

We developed new ways to foster solutions based on a participatory planning framework and involve the community directly in the conversation on the needs and best practices of cities.

Educators

Our team partners with schools and universities to help them design courses and research projects based around review data and community-driven analysis as well as provide data to students and professors conducting research.

The Image of the City

Cities will continually remake themselves and build upwards and out. The question is not how to build the city and fit its residents in, but how to incorporate residents in the city-building conversation from the first step to the last.

Intelligent geospatial applications of review data

City78 delivers a high-touch service to harness big data and natural language processing in the urban planning field to identify and measure trends and narratives in the urban space and correlate these trends to demographics, zoning and factors of the built environment.

All voices heard

Current methods of tracking community perception and use of space such as surveys inherently have blinders on either side of the questions they ask. By leveraging review data, City78 is able to hear all voices, unprompted or directed by questions.

Don't plan 5 years into the future, plan 50

Our team looks for economic and narrative trends in the urban space and in the communities living in these spaces. By placing an emphasis on longitudinal analysis, we build solutions to last.

More on Femi

As an urban planner with close to a decade of experience in demographic studies and urban analysis, Femi has developed an understanding and working knowledge of urban planning tools such as zoning, land use and master planning to ensure economic development and urban growth.

He is skilled in the analysis of statistical data and the identification of factors that determine quality of life. Femi's most recent work includes tracking socioeconomic trends, forecasting accurate development scenarios and analyzing how urban areas are engaging in competition for skilled professionals, locations for cooperation headquarters, prime real estate, and the attraction of tourists and visitors.

More on Theo

Theo Goetemann is a mixed media artist and data scientist. His work frequently explores network and longitudinal data from open data sources and web scrapers. Theo is particularly interested in employing data-driven and narrative analysis techniques to examine online communities. Theo's mixed media pieces deal with interaction with data through physical, three-dimensional spaces and auditory mediums, and are influenced by the idea of making data intuitive to the viewer.

As a supporter of open data, he regularly contributes datasets to data.world. His thesis for the Georgetown Government Honors Program examined the rapid expansion of national open data initiatives globally, which he defended in May 2017, receiving honors with distinction.
