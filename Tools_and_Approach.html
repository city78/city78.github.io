Products & Services

Urban Perception Survey

The best survey your organization never knew it had. As of September 2017, over two-thirds of the world's population is now connected by mobile devices. As this number continues to grow, more and more citizens, tourists and users of the urban space will become intricately connected to the environments they inhabit through their phones.

City78 is the first organization ever to comprehensively survey and analyze entire cities through user-generated data registered on open data and review platforms. Using a mix of traditional urban planning and research techniques along with geospatial clustering algorithms and NLP tools, we bring together the best analytical practices from the urban planning and technology fields to create urban analysis reports through in-depth analysis of data a granular level. 

We are able to track community perception of themes experts care about; walkability, public transportation, safety, parking, atmosphere, scenery and history as well as specific topics like tracking homelessness or cases of racism in commercial and public spaces. 

Through our methods of data-driven community and spatial listening, we are able to identify and cluster narratives individuals hold about the cities they inhabit, develop nuanced recommendations based on the public's input, and foresee and mitigate risks identified by the community, long before other methods of urban analyses are able to.

Our methods of evaluating the urban space reconceptualize participatory planning and co-creation. By directly tapping into organic and unprompted user-generated data, City78 breathes life into the city's feedback channels and helps organizations understand the communities around them, both those that are mapped as well as unmapped, yet have organically mapped themselves.

City78’s Retail Marketplace Analysis recognizes the potential that exists in an urban market for specific commercial uses by measuring saturation of the current locations, as well as market potential. This cumulates to the identification existing market retail leakage or surplus.

OUR CORE INNOVATION  is a methodological approach that fully utilizes the wealth of user-generated data being generated more and more everyday. We believe the organic model of a city is becoming increasingly feasible as participation of the urban dweller in online platforms that have spatial implications and physical representations continue to rise; our methodology makes it possible to strengthen the feedback mechanism between residents and the city, its planners and policymakers.  

Specifically, our core innovation is a combination of geospatial clustering analysis of user-generated review data targeting sentiment and place-locator word frequencies, community outreach and traditional urban planning methods. While our technical methodology may move the needle in terms of making participatory planning an easily-accessible form of analysis, our ideas are rooted in decades of literature and surveys, place imageability analysis and other methods of assessing the needs and perception of communities.  

Jane Jacobs’ assessment of safety and the need for diversity on a street, Kevin Lynch’s Image of the City and conceptualization of imageability, or more contemporary individuals like Eloi Laurent and his arguments for 21st century sustainability through mixed-use communities all advocate for community-driven solutions; City78 has expanded on these professional ideas through a methodological approach to assess communities.
