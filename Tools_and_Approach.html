About City78

Our approach creates avenues for the creation of organic place identities through the co-creation of space and presents a unique opportunity to build cities alongside those who inhabit it.

Our Tools



OUR CORE INNOVATION  is a methodological approach that fully utilizes the wealth of user-generated data being generated more and more everyday. We believe the organic model of a city is becoming increasingly feasible as participation of the urban dweller in online platforms that have spatial implications and physical representations continue to rise; our methodology makes it possible to strengthen the feedback mechanism between residents and the city, its planners and policymakers.  

Specifically, our core innovation is a combination of geospatial clustering analysis of user-generated review data targeting sentiment and place-locator word frequencies, community outreach and traditional urban planning methods. While our technical methodology may move the needle in terms of making participatory planning an easily-accessible form of analysis, our ideas are rooted in decades of literature and surveys, place imageability analysis and other methods of assessing the needs and perception of communities.  

Jane Jacobs’ assessment of safety and the need for diversity on a street, Kevin Lynch’s Image of the City and conceptualization of imageability, or more contemporary individuals like Eloi Laurent and his arguments for 21st century sustainability through mixed-use communities all advocate for community-driven solutions; City78 has expanded on these professional ideas through a methodological approach to assess communities.

Census, Esri Demographics & Open Data

At every step of the way, data that provides insight on the history and trends of the living environment and its population is at the core of all our analyses.  

Review Data

City78 is among the first to fully utilize review data in the urban planning space. It is an untapped source of community-driven suggestions on the best and worst aspects of an urban environment, and provide deep and nuanced insight into the communities around us.

Community Engagement

We believe that the best solutions are the ones that listen to the users of a space. Input from members of the community and discussions with community leaders is an invaluable asset that is embedded in City78's methodology and vision.

Our approach

Regardless of the project, geographic area or topic, City78 rests its methodological approach on three pillars: urban cluster identification, narrative inquiry, and place image analysis.

Urban Cluster Identification

AGGLOMERATION ECONOMIES are one of the most fascinating economic aspects of cities. With our urban cluster identification process, we are able to pinpoint areas in a city with high commercial activity where users of the space visit locations of corresponding uses. In other words, our team is able to track clusters of public and commercial locations that users of the space associate with one another and visit in conjunction of the others.

By identifying these clusters, tracking them across cities, and incorporating Esri and Census data, we are able to provide developers and city governments insight not only into different types of popular location clusters and room for improvement of stagnant clusters, but also insight into customer segmentation and the users of each cluster.

Narrative Inquiry

WITH REVIEW DATA, natural language processing (NLP), and machine learning (MR), our team is able to identify trends in the narratives held by users of the urban space and develop actionable solutions the issue at hand. We are able to track the core narratives in successful commercial spaces, spaces with high repeat-visits and customer loyalty, logistical issues faced by consumers (e.g. ease of parking), as well as other topics such as tracking cases of food deserts or homelessness throughout a city. 

We are able to cluster all of these topics geographically and create and guide our partners through interactive maps which they themselves can use to explore the city and foster the right questions to ask in building solutions to address the issue at hand.

Place Image Analysis

WE BELIEVE that at the heart of all questions surrounding ethical and sustainable development is a deep and nuanced understanding of organic place identity (OPI). Organic place identity involves identification of elements tha co-contribute to the formulation of urban character or place identity. Elements of place image and identity include urban communities, existing economic activity, historic elements in the built environment and the natural environment. Development projects that do not delve into the OPI of the area of development make themselves vulnerable to lackluster support from the community as well as the possibility of losing the unique essence that informs urban competitive advantage.

  City78 has developed our tools and methodologies specifically around place image analysis and OPI in order to make residents and users of space our co-creators in building economically sustainable and resilient cities for the future.
