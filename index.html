What we do

Mission



Organic place identity development

is essential in achieving a thriving and successful urban space; it is a precursor for attracting tourism, investments, talent and trade in an increasingly globalizing and competitive world.

The economic importance of managing place identity and how it informs urban economic performance is what drives us to design models that define and refine the most effective mix of uses in order to achieve sustainable communities and maximize competitive advantage for urban areas.

How



We are fascinated with the idea of an organic city

, one that breathes, grows, and repairs itself. An organic city is only as effective as its own feedback channels from its respective parts.

City78

aims to bring life to these feedback channels and help organizations understand the communities around them, both those that are mapped as well as those that are unmapped yet have organically mapped themselves, through data-driven community and spatial listening. Using a mix of traditional urban planning and research techniques along with geospatial clustering algorithms and NLP tools, we bring together the best analytical practices from the 20th and 21st centuries to create high-level urban analysis reports through in-depth analysis of data a granular level.

About us

Our team thrives on exploration

across research on best practices in developing mixed-use communities and data-driven insights. With our collective experience in urban planning, data science and research, we are prepared to develop effective urban systems, forecast accurate development scenarios, and design original urban spaces that will increase the quality of life for urban populations.


  • Femi

    • As an urban planner with close to a decade of experience in demographic studies and urban analysis, Femi has developed an understanding and working knowledge of urban planning tools such as zoning, land use and master planning to ensure economic development and urban growth.

    He is skilled in the analysis of statistical data and the identification of factors that determine quality of life. Femi's most recent work includes tracking socioeconomic trends, forecasting accurate development scenarios and analyzing how urban areas are engaging in competition for skilled professionals, locations for cooperation headquarters, prime real estate, and the attraction of tourists and visitors.


  • Michael

    • Michael is a researcher with a background in international history. His current research focuses on the various mechanisms that have existed in history and have been employed by societies to reconcile and synthesize different perspectives of their citizens.

    Michael wrote original research on the United States’ decision to cross the 38th parallel during the Korean War, and has worked at Stanford's International Relations Research College with Dr. Naimark as a research assistant. Michael holds a BA in International History from Stanford University.


  • Theo

    • Theo is a mixed media and data visualization artist. His visualizations frequently analyze and explore network and longitudinal data from open data sources and web scrapers. Theo's mixed media pieces deal with interaction with data through physical, three-dimensional spaces and auditory mediums, and are influenced by the idea of making data intuitive to the viewer as well as his interest in biomimicry.

    As a supporter of open data, he regularly contributes datasets to data.world. His thesis for the Georgetown Government Honors Program examined the rapid expansion of national open data initiatives globally, which he defended in May 2017, receiving honors with distinction.
